POSITION MANUAL

Follow how the clothing appears: from sketch to your wardrobe. The process, the materials, the details.

Oxford Shirt — First Sketch

The idea was simple: a shirt that feels like a second skin. Egyptian cotton 200s, French seams, mother-of-pearl buttons. No visible logos — only the owner knows. Every line on paper was drawn with one question: "Does this serve the wearer?"

We rejected 23 prototypes before finding the perfect fit. The sleeve angle, the shoulder slope, the collar height — all tested on real men, not mannequins.

Fabric Selection

We spent months finding the perfect cotton. Only the longest fibers, only from the Nile Delta. The result: a fabric that breathes, drapes, and ages beautifully. Each roll is inspected by hand — any imperfection, and it's rejected.

The cloth is then washed in natural spring water, softening it without chemicals. This is why a POSITION shirt feels broken-in from day one.

The Details

Reinforced elbows — not because they'll wear out, but because a well-made shirt should last decades. The seam on the back follows your spine, allowing the shirt to move with you, not against you.

Buttons are mother-of-pearl, hand-selected for their luster. On the inside, a hidden stitch — the POSITION signature, visible only when you turn the cuff. A reminder that true quality is never loud.
